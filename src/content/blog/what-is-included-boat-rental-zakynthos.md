Before you book a boat rental in Zakynthos, it is worth knowing exactly what you are getting for your money. The inclusions vary depending on the type of rental you choose, but there are a number of things that come as standard with every booking. Knowing what is and is not included helps you plan your day properly and avoid any surprises at the port.

## What Is Always Included

Regardless of whether you go for a self-drive rental or a skipper option, every boat comes equipped with the essentials for a comfortable and safe day on the water.

### Safety equipment

Every boat leaves the port with a full set of legally required safety equipment on board:

- Life jackets for all passengers
- GPS tracking device
- Emergency signalling equipment
- First aid kit

This is non-negotiable. No boat goes out without it, and the team checks everything before departure.

### On-board comfort

Beyond safety, the boats come with a set of extras that make the day genuinely enjoyable rather than just functional:

- Coolbox with ice, ready for your drinks and food
- Bluetooth speaker for music on the water
- Sun canopy to keep you shaded during the hottest part of the day
- Sunbathing area for when you want to anchor and relax

### Safety briefing

For self-drive rentals, a mandatory safety briefing at the port is included before departure. It covers boat controls, navigation basics, the areas you can access, and what to do in an emergency. It takes around 15 to 20 minutes and is conducted in English.

### Meet at the port

The rental includes a proper meet and handover at the port. The team goes over the boat with you, answers any last-minute questions, and makes sure you are comfortable before you set off.

## What Is Not Included

### Fuel

Fuel is not included in the rental price for self-drive bookings. You pay for fuel separately, based on how much you actually use during your trip. The team will explain the refuelling process before departure. Factor this into your budget, especially if you are planning a longer route.

### Food and drinks

The coolbox is provided, but what goes inside it is up to you. Stock up before you arrive at the port. There are supermarkets and shops in Zakynthos Town and near the main ports where you can grab everything you need for a day on the water.

### Snorkelling gear

Snorkelling equipment is not included as standard. If you plan to snorkel at the Keri Caves, Blue Caves, or any of the swimming stops along your route, bring your own mask and fins or hire gear from a local shop before your rental.

## What to Bring With You

To make the most of your rental, here is what we recommend packing:

- Sunscreen, hat, and sunglasses
- Swimwear and a towel
- Drinks and snacks for the day
- Waterproof phone case or dry bag
- Snorkelling gear if you plan to use it
- Cash or card for fuel payment

## FAQ

**Is the safety briefing mandatory for self-drive rentals?** Yes. Every self-drive rental starts with a briefing at the port. It is short, straightforward, and covers everything you need to know before departure.

**Do I need to return the boat with a full tank of fuel?** The team will explain the fuel arrangement at the start of your rental. The standard process involves paying for the fuel used during your trip.

**Can I bring my own food and drinks on board?** Absolutely. The coolbox with ice is there for exactly this reason. Pack whatever you want to eat and drink for the day.

**Is the sun canopy enough shade for a full day?** For most people, yes. If you are particularly sensitive to sun or travelling with young children, bring extra sun protection and plan your route to include anchor stops during the peak midday heat.

**Does the GPS mean the team can track my location during the rental?** The GPS is primarily a safety feature. It ensures that if anything goes wrong, the team can locate the boat quickly. It also helps keep the fleet within the permitted sailing areas.
